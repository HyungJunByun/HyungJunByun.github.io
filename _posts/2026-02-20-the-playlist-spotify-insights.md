# The Playlist
## 5 Insights Learned from Spotify's Founding Story

### 1. A Clear Vision
Spotify founder Daniel Ek was inspired during a time when the illegal music site 'The Pirate Bay' was disrupting the music industry. He established a clear vision: "a legal online music streaming service where music plays within 0.2 seconds." 

The industry couldn't understand his vision, but its clarity gave him and his team the passion to constantly challenge themselves, even through hardships. Furthermore, unlike his teammates who were willing to compromise at 0.4 seconds, Daniel held fast to his 0.2-second vision, which was ultimately realized. 

A clear vision like this is the driving force that keeps a startup team running.

### 2. Startups Are Built by People
In the early days of the startup, Daniel maintained the attitude that the coder was more important than himself. Thanks to this, the genius coder Andreas was able to realize Daniel's vision. Additionally, Petra, the lawyer brought in by co-founder Martin Lorentzon, laid the legal foundation that made today's Spotify possible. 

As such, Spotify's success was not achieved by the founder alone, but together with the people around him. However, after the small successes early on, Daniel became too absorbed in "playing the boss"â€”as Andreas put itâ€”and failed to take care of his people. 

Eventually, he parted ways with coder Andreas and co-founder Martin, which ultimately brought a crisis to Spotify. Both the success and the crises of a startup are brought about by people.

### 3. You Have to Be Crazy to Succeed
> "Only 5% of the world's population has a mental illness, but in Silicon Valley, it's 30%." - Peter Thiel

This is what Peter Thiel of the "PayPal Mafia" said when Martin sought him out for investment. True to these words, Martin Lorentzon consistently showed a lack of seriousness and social skills throughout the series. However, thanks to that very personality, Spotify was able to achieve innovation without yielding to surrounding opposition.

Innovation is possible when you think and act outside the bounds of normality. It becomes evident when observing that Koreansâ€”including myselfâ€”who are adept at reading the room and caring about others' opinions, may be strong in competition but often struggle to achieve true innovation. If you want to achieve innovation rather than just win competitions, you have to break away from the ordinary and be a little crazy.

### 4. But Is Success Sweet Enough to Go Crazy For?
> "It's like Iâ€™ve been dancing, spinning, and flipping upside down my whole life without any fear, as always. But suddenly, someone tells me that everything Iâ€™ve been doing was walking on a slack tightrope." - Martin Lorentzon

In co-founder Martin Lorentzon's retrospective, he confesses that while his lack of social skills helped him succeed, it was simultaneously a dangerous gamble. When Taylor Swift demanded fair compensation and boycotted Spotify, Martin insisted on a hardline response, arguing that she couldn't receive special treatment. It was a very "Martin-esque" choice, far from a typical, moderate compromise. 

However, this choice brought significant losses to Spotify, and ultimately, Martin was betrayed by Daniel and forced to step down from his leadership position. Daniel, too, responded forcefully to protests demanding a larger share of Spotify's profits for the artists. This decision turned his classmate and favorite singer, Bobbi T, into an enemy. 

He achieved massive success, but he lost both his friends and his social reputation. Martin and Daniel were able to succeed because they were crazy in certain aspects. But the price they paid was incredibly steep.

### 5. In the End, What Remains Are People and Experiences
My dream is to start a business. I like the money, honor, and social influence that come with a successful startup. However, I believe that what's even more valuable are the experiences gained and the people met during the founding process. Therefore, while I want to be deeply driven in a specific field like Martin and Daniel, I want to achieve a success that exerts a positive influence on those around me.
